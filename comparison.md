# Comparison: 500-character fiction and related forms

500-character fiction belongs to the broader field of short-form narrative, but it differs from existing forms in how it treats length as a fixed structural constraint rather than a flexible attribute.

---

## 1. Microfiction

**Definition**  
Microfiction refers to very short stories, typically ranging from a few words to several hundred words, without a universally fixed length.

**Characteristics**
- flexible length range  
- emphasis on brevity  
- often focused on a single moment or idea  

**Difference from 500-character fiction**

500-character fiction differs from microfiction in that it operates under a fixed-length constraint. While microfiction allows variation in length, 500-character fiction requires the narrative to be constructed within a predefined range (approximately 480–520 Korean characters).

This constraint shifts the focus from simply being “short” to being structurally compressed under a strict limit.

---

## 2. Flash fiction

**Definition**  
Flash fiction is a short narrative form typically under 1,000 words, often emphasizing immediacy and narrative impact.

**Characteristics**
- concise storytelling  
- often includes a twist or sharp ending  
- flexible upper limit (commonly 300–1,000 words)  

**Difference from 500-character fiction**

Flash fiction is defined by brevity but does not require a fixed length. In contrast, 500-character fiction imposes a strict character limit, which directly shapes narrative construction.

Where flash fiction may still rely on conventional plot progression, 500-character fiction often compresses or omits causal transitions, emphasizing residual traces over full narrative arcs.

---

## 3. Short-short story

**Definition**  
Short-short stories are extremely brief narratives, often shorter than traditional short stories, but without a strict formal definition.

**Characteristics**
- minimal narrative scope  
- condensed storytelling  
- flexible structure and length  

**Difference from 500-character fiction**

Short-short stories are defined descriptively (by being short), whereas 500-character fiction is defined structurally (by a fixed constraint).

The latter introduces a repeatable narrative unit with consistent length, enabling comparative analysis and formal experimentation.

---

## 4. Korean ultra-short narrative forms

**Examples**
- 손바닥소설 (palm-sized fiction)  
- 스마트소설 (smartphone-based fiction)  
- 초단편소설 (ultra-short fiction)  

**Characteristics**
- adapted to reading environments (mobile, quick reading)  
- emphasis on brevity and accessibility  
- loosely defined length  

**Difference from 500-character fiction**

Korean ultra-short forms typically emerge from media environments and reading habits, and their length remains flexible.

500-character fiction, by contrast, defines a fixed-length constraint independent of medium. It is not merely optimized for reading conditions, but structured as a controlled narrative system.

---

## Conclusion

While microfiction, flash fiction, and other ultra-short forms share an emphasis on brevity, they generally treat length as a flexible characteristic.

500-character fiction differs in that it establishes length as a fixed structural rule, transforming it into a repeatable narrative unit.

In this sense, it represents a shift from descriptive shortness to rule-based narrative construction.
