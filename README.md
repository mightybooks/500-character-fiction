# 500-character fiction

500-character fiction is a constrained micro-fiction format proposed by Korean writer Mun Surim.

It is designed as a repeatable narrative unit for creative training and literary experimentation.  
A typical piece contains approximately **480–520 Korean characters**, maintaining a core constraint of about 500 characters.

## Definition

500-character fiction belongs to the broader family of microfiction and flash fiction, but it introduces a fixed character-length constraint that shapes narrative density and structure.

Rather than relying on dramatic plot progression, the format often focuses on the residual traces of events — environments, objects, gestures, and fragments of perception.

## Narrative Approach

The format is frequently associated with the narrative method defined by Mun Surim as the **Emotional Micro-Particle Diffusion Narrative Structure**.

In this structure:

- emotions are not directly explained
- narrative meaning emerges from environmental traces
- readers infer emotional states through observation of post-event conditions

## Character Range

Typical length: 480–520 Korean characters

This margin allows practical flexibility while maintaining the core structural constraint of approximately 500 characters.

## Origin

The format was developed through a series of literary experiments and projects led by Mun Surim, including:

- 500-character fiction writing experiments
- the Surim Studio writing projects
- web-based micro-fiction challenges
